## 1. Suno Official Capabilities

### Official API Status (as of March 2026)

Suno does **not** have a publicly available official API. They have indicated plans for developer access and SDKs, and have rolled out beta access to select partners, but there is no public API key or endpoint system.

The sanctioned ways to use Suno are:
- **Suno Web App** (suno.com) -- manual generation via browser
- **Suno Studio** -- their "Generative Audio Workstation" with multi-track editing, stem separation, MIDI export, and advanced features (Pro/Premier only)

### Model Versions

| Model | Release | Key Features |
|-------|---------|--------------|
| v4.0 | 2024 | Improved genre accuracy, full songs |
| v4.5 | Jul 2025 | Add Vocals/Instrumental, fuller mix |
| v5 | Sep 2025 | Crystal-clear 44.1kHz stereo, near-human vocals, eliminated background haze, superior prompt adherence, ELO score 1293 (vs 1208 for v4.5) |
| v5 Studio (Feb 2026) | Feb 2026 | Warp Markers, Remove FX, Alternates, Time Signature support |

### Pricing & Credits

| Plan | Monthly Cost | Credits/Month | Songs (~) | Commercial Use | WAV Download | Stems |
|------|-------------|---------------|-----------|----------------|--------------|-------|
| Free | $0 | 50/day | ~10/day | No | No (MP3 only) | No |
| Pro | $10/mo ($8/mo annual) | 2,500 | ~500 | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Premier | $30/mo ($24/mo annual) | 10,000 | ~2,000 | Yes | Yes | Yes |

Each song generation consumes ~5 credits. Extensions also consume credits.

### Audio Output

- **MP3**: 192 Kbps, 48 kHz (all plans)
- **WAV**: 48 kHz stereo, lossless (Pro/Premier only)
- **Stems**: Up to 12 time-aligned WAV stems via Studio (drums, bass, vocals, other, etc.)
- **MIDI**: Export available in Studio

### Suno Sounds (SFX Feature)

Suno has a **Suno Sounds** feature (beta) specifically for sound effects:

- **One Shot mode**: Single, short sound effect
- **Loop mode**: Seamless repeating clip
- **Controls**: BPM, musical key
- **Output**: Two samples per generation for comparison
- **Duration**: Can be specified in prompt (e.g., "5 second long sound of...")
- **Limitations**: Still in beta; loops may include full musical arrangements; quality can be inconsistent; iteration required

This means Suno _can_ generate short SFX clips, but it is not its primary strength. Results are hit-or-miss for precise game SFX (1-2 second UI clicks, projectile sounds, etc.).

### Extend Feature

- Available on all plans
- Extends clips up to 1 minute per extension
- Can extend from any timestamp within existing clip
- Maintains style, tempo, and key of original
- Can chain extensions: extend -> extend the extension -> etc.
- "Get Whole Song" stitches extensions together

### Commercial Rights

- **Free plan**: Non-commercial only
- **Pro/Premier**: Commercial use license granted. You can use generated music in games, videos, podcasts, etc.
- **Ownership caveat**: Even with commercial rights, "you generally are not considered the owner of the songs, since the output was generated by Suno" (as of late 2025 policy shift)
- **Warner Music deal** (Nov 2025): Suno announced "more advanced and licensed models" and "monthly download caps" coming
- **Recommendation**: Keep records of all generations. The legal landscape for AI-generated music is still evolving.

## 2. Unofficial Suno API Wrappers

### Overview

All unofficial wrappers use **cookie-based authentication** -- you log into suno.com in a browser, extract the session cookie, and provide it to the wrapper. This is inherently fragile: Suno website updates can (and have) broken authentication.

> **WARNING**: Using unofficial APIs likely violates Suno's Terms of Service. There are risks of account suspension, and any Suno platform change can break these tools overnight. Use for experimentation only, not production pipelines.

### Reliability Assessment

- **gcui-art/suno-api** is the most popular but has had persistent auth issues since January 2025 when Suno changed their cookie naming conventions
- All wrappers are vulnerable to Suno platform changes -- "any Suno website update can break it"
- Some third-party hosted API providers (sunoapi.org, piapi.ai) wrap these tools but charge per-generation and add their own reliability issues
- AI/ML API (aimlapi.com) dropped their Suno integration entirely due to "inability to maintain stated quality standards due to constant problems from Suno AI"

### Recommendation

**Do not build a production pipeline on unofficial Suno APIs.** Instead:
1. Use the **Suno web app manually** for music generation (it is the most reliable path)
2. Use Suno Studio for post-processing (stem separation, editing)
3. Consider using the unofficial APIs only for batch experimentation or prototyping, accepting that they may break

## 3. Alternative Tools for SFX & Audio

### ElevenLabs Sound Effects API

**Best option for game SFX generation.**

- **Website**: https://elevenlabs.io/sound-effects
- **API Docs**: https://elevenlabs.io/docs/overview/capabilities/sound-effects
- **Model**: Sound Effects V2 (Sep 2025)
- **Output**: MP3 (default), WAV at 48 kHz professional quality
- **Duration**: 0.1 seconds minimum, 30 seconds maximum per generation
- **Looping**: Native seamless loop support -- "creates sound effects that can be played on repeat without perceptible start/end points"
- **Pricing**: 200 credits per auto-duration generation, or 40 credits/second for specified duration. Free plan: ~2,500-10,000 credits/month (non-commercial). Starter plan: $5/mo.
- **Commercial**: Royalty-free on paid plans
- **Prompt quality**: Understands audio terminology ("braam", "whoosh", "glitch", "drone"); supports simple descriptions or multi-part sequences; has "prompt influence" control (high=literal, low=creative)

**Why it works for Ojibwe TD**: Short, precise SFX (tower firing, creep death, UI clicks, projectile impacts) with exact duration control. The loop feature is ideal for ambient background sounds.

### jsfxr / sfxr.me

**Best option for retro/procedural game SFX.**

- **Website**: https://sfxr.me/
- **GitHub**: https://github.com/chr15m/jsfxr
- **npm**: `jsfxr` -- can be used as a JavaScript library for runtime sound generation
- **Type**: Procedural 8-bit/retro sound synthesizer
- **Output**: WAV
- **Cost**: Free, open source
- **Capabilities**: Pickup, explosion, laser, jump, hit, blip sounds via parameter randomization
- **Pro version**: Save sound packs, batch WAV export

**Why it works for Ojibwe TD**: The current game already uses WebAudio procedural synthesis (AudioManager.ts). jsfxr could complement or replace this with more polished retro-style SFX. It can also be used as an npm library for runtime generation, avoiding the need to ship audio files.

### Meta AudioCraft (MusicGen + AudioGen)

**Best open-source option for self-hosted generation.**

- **GitHub**: https://github.com/facebookresearch/audiocraft
- **License**: MIT
- **MusicGen**: Text-to-music, trained on 20,000 hours of licensed music
- **AudioGen**: Text-to-environmental-sounds and SFX (distinct model)
- **Requirements**: GPU required (CUDA), Python, significant compute resources
- **Quality**: Good but not Suno-tier for music; AudioGen is solid for environmental/ambient sounds

**Why it might work**: If you want fully self-hosted, no-API-dependency audio generation. But requires GPU and is more complex to set up than cloud alternatives.

### Stability AI - Stable Audio

- **Website**: https://stableaudio.com/
- **Open model**: Stable Audio Open on Hugging Face (up to 47 seconds, trained on Freesound + Free Music Archive)
- **Commercial model**: Stable Audio 2.5 -- up to 3 minutes, API available via Replicate
- **Capabilities**: Music, SFX, drum beats, ambient sounds, foley
- **Quality**: Good for ambient textures and environmental sounds

### Freesound.org

**Best option for curated, pre-made Creative Commons SFX.**

- **Website**: https://freesound.org/
- **API**: RESTful API (Freesound APIv2) -- search, filter by license, download, content-based similarity search
- **Collection**: 230,000+ sounds, CC-licensed
- **Formats**: MP3, FLAC, OGG, WAV
- **Cost**: Free for limited API use; commercial license required for heavy API usage
- **Usefulness**: Great for ambient nature sounds, environmental audio, UI sounds, foley

### Other Notable Mentions

- **Soundverse AI** (soundverse.ai) -- API-driven music generation with stem separation; supports Suno-like features
- **Bark** (suno-ai/bark on GitHub) -- Suno's own open-source text-to-audio model; can generate speech, music, sound effects, but older and less polished than current Suno
- **Riffusion** -- Spectogram-based music generation; interesting but less practical for game audio

## 4. Suno Prompt Engineering for Game Audio

### Prompt Structure Formula

```
[genre] + [tempo-feel] + [instrumentation] + [vocal intent] + [mix/production] + [mood]
```

For game music, always include:
- `instrumental` or `no vocals` (preferably both)
- Purpose context: "game soundtrack", "tower defense ambient", "menu theme"
- Loop intent: "seamless loop", "loop-friendly", "loop-ready"

### Instrumental-Only Techniques

Three methods should be combined for best results:

1. **Style prompt**: Include "instrumental, no vocals" near the beginning
2. **Lyrics field**: Leave empty OR enter only `[Instrumental]`
3. **Negative prompting** (v5): "no vocals, no singing, no humming, no choir, no voice"

### Genre/Style Tags for Ojibwe TD

#### For Main Menu / Title Theme
```
epic ambient world music, instrumental, no vocals, wooden flute melody,
deep resonant drums, string drones, nature atmosphere, 90 BPM,
warm analog tone, cinematic, wide stereo image, seamless loop
```

#### For Gameplay / In-Game Background
```
ambient electronic world fusion, instrumental, no vocals, soft pentatonic melody,
gentle percussion, atmospheric pads, subtle tension, 100 BPM,
clean mix high fidelity, game soundtrack, seamless loop, evolving layers
```

#### For Boss Waves
```
dark ambient orchestral, instrumental, no vocals, deep war drums,
ominous drone bass, tense strings, building intensity, 120 BPM,
cinematic epic, dramatic, high energy, no strong vocals
```

#### For Game Over / Defeat
```
melancholic ambient, instrumental, no vocals, solo wooden flute,
gentle reverb, soft pads, reflective, 60 BPM, emotional depth,
fading, minimal, clean mix
```

#### For Victory / Wave Complete
```
uplifting world ambient, instrumental, no vocals, celebratory flute melody,
warm strings, gentle drums, triumphant, 110 BPM, bright,
nature sounds, hopeful, short fanfare
```

| Category | Tags |

### Instrument Tags Relevant to the Game's Theme

| Instrument | Suno Tag | Notes |
|------------|----------|-------|

| Hand drum | "hand drum", "frame drum", "deep resonant drums" | Rhythmic heartbeat |

### Loop Creation Tips

1. Include "seamless loop" and "soft reverb tail" in prompt
2. End each section on the same chord/tonal center as the start
3. Use the Extend feature to build longer loops, then crossfade in an editor
4. Request 4-8 bar phrase structures for cleaner loop points
5. Post-process with ffmpeg crossfade (see Workflow section)
6. If looping is hit-or-miss, generate multiple takes and pick the best one

### Multi-Pass Building Strategy

Rather than one complex prompt, use Suno Studio's multi-track approach:
1. **Pass 1**: Foundation (texture + ambient pad only)
2. **Pass 2**: Add cultural identity instruments (flute, drums)
3. **Pass 3**: Add subtle movement/rhythm
4. **Pass 4**: Texture refinements
5. **Pass 5**: Extend with callback phrasing for looping
6. **Pass 6**: Light remaster only

## 5. Respectful Approach to Indigenous-Inspired Audio

### Core Principles

The game is created by an Ojibwe person, which provides important context. However, care should still be taken:

1. **Avoid sacred/ceremonial music elements**: Do not use or imitate specific ceremonial songs, powwow protocols, or sacred musical patterns. These belong to specific communities and contexts.

2. **Draw from general musical characteristics, not specific songs**: Use pentatonic scales, wooden flute timbres, drum rhythms as _inspiration_ without copying specific traditional pieces.

3. **Ojibwe musical elements to draw from (non-sacred)**:
   - The **shaker/rattle** as rhythmic foundation (the "first instrument" of Ojibwe people)
   - The **drum** as communal heartbeat (frame drums, water drums)
   - The **wooden flute** ("a bec" style) for melody
   - Pentatonic scales and simple, grounded rhythmic patterns
   - Nature-connected themes (water, wind, forest, seasons)

4. **What to avoid in Suno prompts**:
   - Do NOT use terms like "tribal", "primitive", "exotic", "jungle drums"
   - Do NOT request powwow singing, honor songs, or ceremony music
   - Do NOT use "Native American chanting" or similar reductive stereotypes
   - AVOID the "drums + flute = indigenous" cliche without nuance

5. **Better prompt approach**: Frame prompts around natural elements and specific instruments rather than cultural labels:
   - Instead of "tribal Native American music" -> "ambient world music, wooden flute, frame drum, nature atmosphere, pentatonic melody"
   - Instead of "indigenous chanting" -> "warm vocal harmonics, no lyrics, humming tones" (or just use instrumental)

6. **Consider crediting and consulting**: The game's "notes" section should credit the musical tradition and the creator's own heritage connection. Consider consulting with other Ojibwe musicians or cultural advisors if using specific cultural elements beyond general atmospheric inspiration.

### Suno Prompt Patterns (Respectful)

```
// DO: Ground in nature and specific instruments
"ambient atmospheric, wooden flute melody, gentle frame drum,
 forest atmosphere, water sounds, warm organic, pentatonic,
 instrumental, no vocals, peaceful, 80 BPM"

// DON'T: Use reductive cultural stereotypes
"tribal native american drums chanting ritual music"

// DO: Use world music fusion that honors without appropriating
"ambient world fusion, organic instruments, breathy flute,
 deep resonant hand drum, nature field recordings,
 meditative, earthy, warm analog, instrumental"

// DON'T: Exoticize
"exotic jungle tribal drums primitive indigenous"
```

## 6. Workflow Patterns & Integration

### Recommended End-to-End Workflow

```
1. GENERATE (Suno web app, manual)
   |
   v
2. DOWNLOAD (WAV from Suno, Pro plan)
   |
   v
3. POST-PROCESS (ffmpeg / Audacity)
   - Trim to loop points
   - Crossfade for seamless loops
   - Normalize loudness (EBU R128)
   - Convert to target format
   |
   v
4. ORGANIZE (game/public/assets/audio/)
   - music/   -> background tracks (OGG or MP3, ~128kbps)
   - sfx/     -> sound effects (MP3 or WAV, short)
   - ambient/ -> ambient loops (OGG or MP3)
   |
   v
5. INTEGRATE (Phaser 3 / Web Audio)
   - Use existing AudioManager.ts or Phaser Sound Manager
   - Preload in BootScene
   - Play/loop in GameScene
```

### ffmpeg Post-Processing Commands

#### Trim audio to specific duration
```bash
ffmpeg -i input.wav -ss 0 -t 30 -c copy output.wav
```

#### Create seamless loop with crossfade
```bash
# Crossfade last 2 seconds with first 2 seconds
ffmpeg -i input.wav -filter_complex \
  "[0]atrim=end=28[main];[0]atrim=start=28[tail];[0]atrim=end=2[head];\
   [tail][head]acrossfade=d=2:c1=tri:c2=tri[fade];\
   [main][fade]concat=n=2:v=0:a=1" \
  output_loop.wav
```

#### Normalize loudness (EBU R128)
```bash
# Using ffmpeg-normalize (pip install ffmpeg-normalize)
ffmpeg-normalize input.wav -o output.wav -t -14 -tp -1
```

#### Convert to OGG for web (good compression, wide browser support)
```bash
ffmpeg -i input.wav -c:a libvorbis -q:a 5 output.ogg
```

#### Convert to MP3 for web fallback
```bash
ffmpeg -i input.wav -c:a libmp3lame -b:a 128k output.mp3
```

#### Batch process a directory
```bash
for f in *.wav; do
  ffmpeg -i "$f" -c:a libvorbis -q:a 5 "${f%.wav}.ogg"
done
```

### Phaser 3 Audio Integration

The current game uses a custom `AudioManager.ts` with raw WebAudio API (OscillatorNode + GainNode). For file-based audio:

**Option A: Use Phaser's built-in Sound Manager**
```typescript
// In BootScene or preload:
this.load.audio('menu-music', ['assets/audio/music/menu-theme.ogg', 'assets/audio/music/menu-theme.mp3']);
this.load.audio('game-music', ['assets/audio/music/game-bg.ogg', 'assets/audio/music/game-bg.mp3']);
this.load.audio('sfx-tower-fire', ['assets/audio/sfx/tower-fire.ogg']);

// In GameScene:
const music = this.sound.add('game-music', { loop: true, volume: 0.5 });
music.play();

// SFX:
this.sound.play('sfx-tower-fire', { volume: 0.8 });
```

**Option B: Extend existing AudioManager.ts**
Add file playback alongside procedural synthesis:
```typescript
// Load audio buffers via fetch + decodeAudioData
// Play via AudioBufferSourceNode connected to existing gain chain
// This preserves the existing mute/volume/gain infrastructure
```

**Option C: Use Howler.js alongside Phaser**
```typescript
import { Howl } from 'howler';
const bgMusic = new Howl({
  src: ['assets/audio/music/game-bg.ogg', 'assets/audio/music/game-bg.mp3'],
  loop: true, volume: 0.5, html5: true  // html5: true for large files
});
bgMusic.play();
```

**Recommendation**: Option B (extend AudioManager.ts) is best -- it preserves the existing mute/volume/gain chain, keeps a single audio context, and avoids adding a new dependency.

### Web Audio Gotchas

- **Autoplay policy**: Browsers require user interaction before audio can play. The current AudioManager already handles this with `document.addEventListener('pointerdown', ctx.resume, { once: true })`
- **Single AudioContext**: Avoid creating multiple AudioContexts. Route all audio through one.
- **Memory**: Decode audio buffers once, reuse them. Don't re-fetch on every play.
- **Format support**: OGG Vorbis has the best quality/size ratio and works in all modern browsers except Safari (use MP3 fallback)
- **Loop gapless**: For music loops, use `AudioBufferSourceNode.loop = true` with `loopStart` and `loopEnd` properties for sample-accurate looping (better than Phaser's built-in loop)

## 9. Gotchas & Warnings

### Suno API Wrappers

- **DO NOT rely on unofficial APIs for production workflows**. They break regularly when Suno updates their platform.
- The gcui-art/suno-api wrapper has had persistent authentication failures since January 2025 due to cookie naming convention changes.
- AI/ML API dropped their Suno integration entirely due to reliability problems.
- Using automated wrappers risks account suspension under Suno's TOS.
- Many wrapper users "overpay by 50-70% by using inefficient wrappers" compared to direct Suno use.

### Legal / Licensing

- AI-generated music copyright is legally uncertain. Major label lawsuits (Sony, Universal, Warner vs Suno, June 2024) are ongoing.
- Suno's late 2025 policy change means you may have commercial USE rights but not full OWNERSHIP of generated audio.
- The Warner Music deal (Nov 2025) suggests Suno's licensing terms may change further.
- **Recommendation**: Keep records of all prompts and generations. Use Suno Pro for commercial rights. Consider this a "good enough for indie games" situation but monitor the legal landscape.

### Audio Quality

- Suno MP3 downloads are 192 Kbps -- adequate for games but not studio quality. Always download WAV when possible (Pro plan).
- Suno may introduce unwanted vocals even when "instrumental" is specified. Always review output and regenerate if needed.
- Loop quality from Suno is inconsistent. Plan to do loop-point editing in Audacity or ffmpeg.
- ElevenLabs SFX can sometimes produce artifacts at very short durations (< 0.5s).

### Web/Game Integration

- Browser autoplay policies mean audio must be triggered by user interaction. The existing pointerdown resume handler covers this.
- OGG Vorbis is the best web format but Safari requires MP3 fallback. Always provide both formats.
- Large audio files should use `html5: true` (Howler) or streaming to avoid blocking main thread during decode.
- Phaser's Sound Manager is global (survives scene changes) -- explicitly stop looping sounds when leaving scenes.

### Cultural

- Even though the game creator is Ojibwe, avoid using specific sacred or ceremonial music elements in AI-generated audio. AI models may produce inauthentic or disrespectful amalgamations of cultural elements.
- Suno's training data likely includes problematic "Native American" music samples that perpetuate stereotypes. Be specific with instrument names rather than cultural labels.
- Consider having generated audio reviewed by community members before finalizing.
